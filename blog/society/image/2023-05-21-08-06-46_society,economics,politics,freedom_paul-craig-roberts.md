Paul Craig Roberts is an American economist who once worked inside the government. During the Reagan era, he served as an Assistant Secretary of the Treasury. After leaving his post, he became a strong voice against popular political ideas. He states that recent policies damage ordinary citizens.

Roberts opposes modern free trade rules. He argues that global agreements destroy local American jobs and shut down domestic factories. He supports tariffs to protect workers at home instead of letting corporations move overseas. He criticizes the Federal Reserve for printing excess currency. He warns that massive national debt creates dangerous financial risks.

Roberts worries about the growing power of the state. He warns that citizens are losing their basic privacy and civil liberties. He argues against mass surveillance and the abuse of authority. He opposes foreign military actions. In his view, foreign wars help powerful corporate groups rather than the American public.

Roberts shares his ideas with historical thinkers like Ludwig von Mises and Friedrich Hayek. He believes free markets and individual rights form the core of a healthy society. He warns that media companies hide the truth to protect corporate interests. For Roberts, limiting government power protects individual freedom.
