***Witchi Tai To*** is a song by Jim Pepper, a Native American jazz saxophonist of Kaw and Muscogee Creek descent. The song was first released on the 1971 album *Pepper's Pow Wow* and has since been covered by numerous artists, including Brewer & Shipley, Harper's Bizarre, and Jan Garbarek.

The song is based on a peyote chant that Pepper learned from his grandfather. It features a simple and repetitive melody that is both haunting and uplifting. The lyrics are in a combination of English and Kaw, and they express a message of spiritual connection and healing.

Jim Pepper was a pioneering figure in the jazz world, known for his unique blend of traditional Native American music and modern jazz. He was a member of the influential jazz-rock group The Free Spirits and collaborated with many legendary jazz musicians, including Don Cherry, Ornette Coleman, and Charlie Haden.

*Witchi Tai To* is considered to be one of Pepper's most significant works, and it remains a popular and influential track in both the jazz and world music genres. It is often cited as a prime example of the way that traditional music can be reimagined and repurposed for a modern audience.
