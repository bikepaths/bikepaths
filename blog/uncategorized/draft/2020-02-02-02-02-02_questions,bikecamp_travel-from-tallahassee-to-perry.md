From Tallahassee to Perry
53 miles - Cumulative Miles: 1,684  
Route: US 19  
County: Taylor / County Seat: Perry

The journey from Tallahassee to Perry covers 53 miles (85 kilometers) along US 19, traversing Taylor County in northern Florida. This route is characterized by a combination of lush landscapes, rural communities, and a rich ecological backdrop. As you cycle southward, you’ll experience the region’s unique geology and varied terrain, making this leg of the journey both enjoyable and educational.

The topography around Tallahassee gradually transitions into gently rolling hills as you approach Perry. The elevation changes are modest, typically ranging from 60 to 90 feet (18 to 27 meters) above sea level, and are indicative of the region’s geological history, shaped by sedimentary deposits and ancient sea beds. The land is predominantly composed of sandy soils mixed with clay, reflecting the area's history as part of the coastal plain that once supported rich marine environments. 

Cyclists will notice an abundance of pine forests, hardwood swamps, and river systems along the way. The region's vegetation is characteristic of the subtropical climate, showcasing a diversity of flora, including longleaf pines, cypress trees, and various wildflowers. The understory is rich with ferns and shrubs, providing habitat for an array of fauna such as deer, raccoons, and a variety of bird species, including herons and woodpeckers. This biodiversity not only enhances the scenic beauty but also contributes to the overall ecological health of the region.

As you reach Perry, a notable cultural landmark is the Forest Capital State Park, dedicated to celebrating the timber industry’s historical significance in Florida. The park features educational displays about the forestry industry, making it an excellent stop for cyclists interested in the region’s economic history. Within the park, several trails offer opportunities for exploration, and the area is known for its peaceful ambiance, allowing for a reflective pause during your ride.

Nearby, Econfina River State Park provides additional recreational opportunities, including camping and biking facilities. The park’s location along the Econfina River adds to the natural beauty of the area, with clear waters that invite canoeing and fishing. The presence of picnic areas and restroom facilities makes it an ideal stop for cyclists needing a break.

Perry itself offers various amenities for travelers. The Taylor County Public Library is a welcoming place for cyclists to rest, equipped with Wi-Fi and comfortable seating. This space not only allows for a brief respite but also serves as a hub for local information, including maps and resources for further exploration in the area.

Throughout this segment, US 19 maintains wide shoulders and low traffic volumes, ensuring a safe and enjoyable ride for cyclists. The smooth pavement, combined with the scenic views of the surrounding forests and rivers, makes this route a prime choice for biking enthusiasts. In summary, the trip from Tallahassee to Perry provides a unique blend of cultural landmarks, ecological richness, and cycling-friendly infrastructure, making it a rewarding segment of any long-distance journey.
