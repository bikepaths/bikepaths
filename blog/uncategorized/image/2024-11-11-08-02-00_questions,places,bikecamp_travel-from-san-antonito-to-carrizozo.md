#### A Ride Through Time and Terrain

The 53-mile journey from San Antonio to Carrizozo, New Mexico, is a ride through a landscape shaped by fire and time. This stretch of US 380 is a journey into the heart of the high desert, a place of stunning vistas, rich history, and a quiet, powerful beauty. For the cyclist, it is a chance to connect with a unique and captivating part of the American West.

#### The Lay of the Land

The terrain here is a beautiful mix of rolling hills and wide-open plains. The high desert elevation, ranging from 4,500 to 5,300 feet, offers panoramic views of the surrounding mountains and valleys. The air is clear and the sky is vast, creating a sense of spaciousness and freedom. The road itself is well-maintained, with wide shoulders that make for a comfortable and safe ride.

#### A Land Forged in Fire

The geology of this region tells a dramatic story. The landscape has been shaped by the volcanic activity of the Rio Grande rift. Near Carrizozo, you will find the Valley of Fires Recreation Area, home to the Carrizozo Malpais, a massive lava flow that stretches for 44 miles. This dark, basaltic rock creates a stunning contrast with the lighter colors of the desert, a stark and beautiful reminder of the earth’s creative power.

#### A Glimpse into the Past

Carrizozo, the destination of this ride, is a small town with a rich history. The Carrizozo Heritage Museum offers a fascinating look into the town’s past as a bustling railroad hub. The exhibits tell the story of the railroad’s impact on the region, a reminder of the vital role that transportation has always played in shaping the American West.

#### The Life of the High Desert

The high desert is a place of surprising life. The landscape is dotted with hardy plants like yucca, sagebrush, and a variety of cacti, all adapted to thrive in this arid environment. The wildlife is equally resilient, with mule deer, coyotes, and a diverse population of birds making their home here. The quiet observer will be rewarded with glimpses of this vibrant ecosystem.

#### An Oasis on the Road

For the traveling cyclist, Carrizozo is a welcome oasis. The town offers basic services, including restaurants and shops. The Carrizozo Public Library is a wonderful place to rest, recharge, and connect with the digital world. It is a friendly and welcoming stop on a long and beautiful journey.

#### A Journey of Solitude and Beauty

A ride from San Antonio to Carrizozo is a journey into the quiet heart of the New Mexico high desert. It is a chance to experience the vastness of the landscape, to connect with the deep history of the land, and to find a sense of peace in the solitude of the open road. It is a ride that will stay with you long after you have reached your destination.
